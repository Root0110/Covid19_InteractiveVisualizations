Brief Introductions

There is a big event happening, affecting every person in the earth, and it is named Covid-19 by WHO. Up to April 24th, the coronavirus pandemic has seen more than 2,850,000 confirmed cases and more than 198,000 deaths worldwide. Being a member of the humanity, I feel like everyone be equal struggling with the disaster, and the empathy with people in hard time drives me to do something, and the thanksgiving heart of having food and a healthy body enables me to devote what I am learning to do some visualizations and analysis.

The dataset I use records the number of confirmed cases and the number of deaths around the world, daily updated. And I got the dataset containing the most updated records, and there are a column of datetime ranging from 2019-12-31 to 2020-04-24, the number of daily new cases and deaths in each country/region, also the total numbers. And the number are still being updated, since the Covid-19 is still spreading over the world. Here are some interactive visualizations and statistical analysis, allowing readers to explore and learn more about this "disaster".

Visualizations

Figure 1. Global Deaths of Countries

The following interactive data map presents the Covid-19 situation regarding to death quantity in each country, with different colors from light to dark. As you can see, the dark blue areas have more deaths while the light yellow areas have less deaths. And if you put your click on a certain country, the precise number of deaths(till 04/24/2020) would show up.

Views from the author:

  • The north America and the Europe are suffering most from the Covid-19, from the perspective of the total deaths.
  • Countries/regions at the same continent are highly dependent with each other. E.g. In the Western Europe, almost all countries are colored with dark blue even purple, which is consistent to the common sense of infectious diseases' regional transmissions.
  • As a whole, the global deaths situation is not as optimistic as I thought last year. With the globalization being more and more important, it is hard for one country/region to totally isolate itself with others.

    Figure 2. Dashboard to Explore Monthly & Daily Number of Cases in 5 Continents

    This heatmap provides another dimensionality: time. So, readers can form a better understanding of the evolution of the cases and deaths confirmed during these months. In more details, the number of daily cases can be tracked. On the left side of this dashboard, the heatmap presents the number of confirmed cases of each continent, during each month. The darker color the cell has, the more confirmed cases. And when one cell is selected, the string at the top would tell you the precise number, and at the sametime, the bar plot lieing on the right side would present the number of confirmed cases of each continent, during each day. In order to group a large number of records into groups, I add the continent columns by downloading another dataset.

    Views from the author:

  • April is the biggest break-out for all continents, since the total number of confirmed cases in each continent is the largest.
  • In Feburary, there was not showing clear patterns of the growth of the cases' quantity. However, in March, there is a clear rapidly growing trend, that was the time when most contries/regions payed attention and conducted Nucleic acid amplification tests (NAATs) on potential patients.
  • The outbreak in Asia mainly happened in Feb and Mar, while in Americas and Europe, it was in Mar and Apr.

    Figure 3. Trendline of Global Cases and Deaths Added Per Day

  • In this figure, we can have a look at the cases and deaths at the same time.
  • The global outbreak started rougly at 2020-03-15, and achieved a pretty high value at April, and are experiencing some fluctuations.
  • The growth of the number of deaths is much slower than that of confirmed cases, one reason is that the medical cure methods are becomming more and more efficient, as we learned more about the virus.

    Figure 4. Bar Plot of Confirmed Cases in Top 50 Countries

  • The bar plot of countries with top 50 confirmed cases presents that a small portion of countries takes the most part of the global numbers.
  • However, the absolute number is not appropriate to judge directly, since it is closly related to the population of each country.

    Figure 5. Interactive Scatterplots between Varaibles

    It's easy for readers to choose any two meaningful variables, and have a look at the scatterplot to find potential relationship. Also, the colors of data points in the scatterplot would represent another variable, so that more complex connections would be explored.
